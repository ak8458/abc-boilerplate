## 1. Business Goals & Digital Strategy

### Why Are You Considering a New Web Platform?

**Current Challenges:**
- What problems are you experiencing with your current HTML website?
- What takes too long or is too difficult to do today?
- What opportunities are you missing because of website limitations?
- What do your competitors do on their websites that you can't do?

**Business Objectives:**
- What are your main business goals for the next 1-2 years?
- How does your website support your business goals?
- What would success look like for a new website platform?
- What would make this project worth the investment?

**Digital Presence:**
- How important is your website to your business?
- How do customers or visitors use your website?
- Do you have other digital channels? (social media, email, mobile apps)
- How does your website fit into your overall digital strategy?

**Future Vision:**
- Where do you want your website to be in 2-3 years?
- What new capabilities would you like to add over time?
- Are there new markets, products, or services you plan to support?
- How do you expect your content volume to grow?

## 2. Current Website & Content

### Understanding Your Existing Website

**Website Basics:**
- How many websites or web properties do you have?
- Approximately how many pages does your website have?
- When was your current website last updated or redesigned?
- Who built your current website? (internal team, agency, contractor)

**How Your Website Works Today:**
- How do you make changes to your website now?
- Who updates the HTML files?
- Where are your HTML files stored? (local computers, server, cloud)
- How do changes get published to the live site?

**Content Types:**
- What types of content do you publish? (articles, news, products, resources, etc.)
- What types of pages do you have? (home, about, contact, product pages, etc.)
- Do you have any special sections? (blog, news, events, resources)
- How often do you add new pages?

**Current Pain Points:**
- What's the biggest frustration with managing your current website?
- How long does it take to make a simple update?
- What can't you do with your current website that you wish you could?
- Do you need technical skills to make changes?

**Assets & Media:**
- Where do you store images, videos, and documents?
- How many images/files do you have approximately?
- How do you organize your media files?
- Do multiple people need access to these files?

## 3. Your Team & Resources

### Understanding Your Organization

**Team Structure:**
- Who is responsible for the website today?
- Do you have in-house developers or IT staff?
- Who creates or updates website content?
- How many people need to work on the website?

**Technical Skills:**
- What technical skills does your team have?
- Are team members comfortable with tools like Microsoft Word or Google Docs?
- Does your team have experience with version control (like GitHub)?
- What training or support might your team need?

**Roles & Responsibilities:**
- Who will be responsible for keeping the website up-to-date?
- Who approves content before it goes live?
- Who handles technical issues or problems?
- Who makes decisions about website changes?

**External Partners:**
- Do you work with any agencies or contractors?
- Who might you need to hire or contract for this project?
- Do you have vendors for design, development, or hosting?
- What support will you need from Adobe or partners?

**Time & Commitment:**
- How much time can your team dedicate to this project?
- Are there other competing priorities?
- When would be a good time to start this project?
- What would prevent your team from focusing on this?

## 4. Content Creation & Publishing

### How Content Gets Created and Published

**Content Creation Process:**
- Who writes or creates content for your website?
- What tools do they use today? (Word, Google Docs, email, text editor)
- How do they currently send content to get published?
- How many people create content?

**Content Updates:**
- How often do you update content? (daily, weekly, monthly)
- What types of updates are most common? (text changes, new pages, images)
- What's the typical process from "we need to update something" to "it's live"?
- How quickly do you need to make urgent updates?

**Approval & Review:**
- Does content need to be reviewed before going live?
- Who needs to approve content?
- How does the approval process work today?
- How long does approval typically take?

**Publishing Workflow:**
- What's the ideal workflow for creating and publishing content?
- Who should be able to publish directly vs. needing approval?
- Do you need to schedule content to go live at a specific time?
- Do you ever need to take content down or unpublish it?

**Content Collaboration:**
- Do multiple people work on the same content?
- How do you track changes or versions?
- How do you communicate about content updates?
- What challenges do you face when collaborating on content?

## Appendix: How to Use This Guide

### For Discovery Sessions

**Before the Meeting:**
- Review the customer's current website
- Select relevant questions from each category
- Prioritize based on known information
- Allow 2-3 hours for a comprehensive discovery session

**During the Meeting:**
- Start with Business Goals (Section 1) to understand "why"
- Move to Current Website (Section 2) to understand "what exists"
- Cover Team & Resources (Section 3) to understand "who"
- Discuss remaining sections based on priorities
- Take detailed notes
- Ask follow-up questions
- Listen for pain points and opportunities

**After the Meeting:**
- Document key findings
- Identify gaps in understanding
- Note any areas needing deeper exploration
- Share summary with customer for validation

### Question Priorities for First Meeting

**Must Cover:**
1. Why are you considering a new platform? (Business Goals)
2. How many pages and websites do you have? (Current Website)
3. Who will work on the website? (Team & Resources)
4. What's your timeline and budget? (Project Readiness)

**Should Cover:**
5. How do you update content today? (Content Creation)
6. What features do you need? (Website Features)
7. What are your performance goals? (Performance & UX)

### Tips for Success

**Do:**
- Use simple, jargon-free language
- Ask "why" to understand motivations
- Listen for pain points
- Be patient with customers new to CMS concepts
- Educate as you go
- Set realistic expectations

**Don't:**
- Assume CMS knowledge
- Use technical acronyms without explaining
- Rush through questions
- Make assumptions about their needs
- Over-promise capabilities
- Skip foundational questions

### Red Flags to Watch For

- Unrealistic timeline expectations
- Lack of team capacity or resources
- Unclear business objectives
- No decision-making authority in the room
- Resistance to change from key stakeholders
- Overly complex requirements for first phase

### Green Flags to Encourage

- Clear business goals
- Executive sponsorship
- Willingness to start simple
- Team openness to new ways of working
- Realistic timeline and budget
- Clear content ownership

## What is AEM Edge Delivery Services?

### Simple Explanation

Adobe Experience Manager (AEM) Edge Delivery Services is a modern way to build and manage websites that is:

**Fast:** Websites load in under a second, which keeps visitors happy and helps with search rankings

**Easy for Authors:** Content creators can use familiar tools like Microsoft Word or Google Docs instead of learning complex systems

**Developer-Friendly:** Developers can build features quickly using modern tools and best practices

**Low Maintenance:** The system handles updates, security, and scaling automatically

**Cost-Effective:** You only pay for what you use, and it requires less technical resources to maintain

### How It's Different from Your HTML Website

| Your Current HTML Site | With Edge Delivery Services |
|------------------------|---------------------------|
| Edit HTML files directly | Create content in Word/Google Docs |
| Need technical skills to update | Authors can update without developers |
| Manual file management | Automatic publishing and version control |
| Limited collaboration | Multiple people can work together easily |
| Manual deployment | Automatic deployment when you save |
| Hard to keep consistent | Built-in templates and components |
| Performance varies | Consistently fast (100/100 scores) |

### Key Benefits for Your Organization

1. **Faster Content Updates:** Make changes in minutes instead of hours or days
2. **Empower Your Team:** Content creators can work independently
3. **Better Performance:** Faster websites mean happier visitors and better search rankings
4. **Lower Costs:** Less time spent on maintenance and updates
5. **Scalability:** Handle traffic spikes automatically
6. **Modern Foundation:** Built for the future with the latest technology
