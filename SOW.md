3.	OBJECTIVE
For more than 70 years, Alberta Blue Cross (ABS) has been recognized as an organization that delivers quality service and competitive benefits while keeping customers at the heart of every interaction.  ABC’s mission is to provide health and wellness plans, programs and services on a viable, not-for-profile basis for the financial protection and wellbeing of our communities.

ABC is embarking on a journey to act on their digital strategy in delivering exceptional experience, is working on an initiative to enhance the efficiency, flexibility, and collaborative potential of its digital content and asset process enabling a wider range of team members to easily update and manage web content, including digital assets.

ABC is seeking to optimize and accelerate content production with the goal of providing more personalized content and experiences at scale.  Adobe will partner with ABC to lay the foundation for Adobe Experience Manager (AEM) by conducting the following services:

•	Adobe Experience Manager Assets Ultimate Starter
•	Edge Delivery Services Design & Discovery and Migration Accelerator
•	Adobe Experience Manager Content Foundation Service

a.	Customer must notify Adobe in writing of any rejection of a Deliverable within 7 days of delivery of the Deliverable (“Acceptance Testing Period”); this notice must provide Adobe with sufficient information to enable Adobe to identify the non-conformity with the applicable acceptance criteria (“Rejection Notice”).

a.	a "Major Non-conformance" which includes items that affect the functionality of a Deliverable, the absence of which will render the Deliverable un-useable.; and 
b.	a "Minor Non-conformance" which includes items that do not affect the functionality of a Deliverable, the absence of which will not render a Deliverable un-useable.
c.	If a Deliverable is classified as a Major Non-conformance:	
d.	Adobe will remedy the Deliverable at Adobe’s own cost and redeliver the Deliverable to Customer within a reasonable time; and
e.	Following redelivery, Customer may repeat the same acceptance procedure on the remedied Deliverable.

f.	Adobe will use reasonable efforts to remedy the Deliverable; and
g.	These items will not be subject to further acceptance testing and will not postpone Customer's Acceptance or the Completion of the Deliverable.
(G)	Remediation of a Major Non-Conformance.  If Adobe fails to remedy a Deliverable classified as a Major Non-conformance item despite 3 attempts to do so or within a period of 2 months following Customer giving notice of such Major Non-conformance (whichever occurs first), the parties will meet to agree reasonable next steps, and in any event, Customer's sole and exclusive remedy shall be to reject the defective Deliverable and to recover from Adobe any amount paid by Customer for the Deliverable.
