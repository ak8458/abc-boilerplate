## Project Overview

### Objective

## Migration Approach

### Methodology: Content-Driven Development

- **Author-First**: Prioritizes ease of content creation
- **Systematic Workflow**: 5-step process for each page
- **Quality-Assured**: 6 quality gates per page
- **Proven Skills**: Specialized AI-powered migration workflows

### 16-Week Timeline

**Phase 1: Discovery & Planning** (Weeks 1-2)
- Complete site inventory and analysis
- Design content models
- Prepare migration toolkit

**Phase 2: Pilot Migration** (Weeks 3-4)
- Migrate 15-20 representative pages
- Validate workflow and quality
- Go/No-Go decision point

**Phase 3: Bulk Migration - Main Site** (Weeks 5-10)
- Systematic batch migration (~300 pages)
- Continuous quality assurance
- Daily progress tracking

**Phase 4: Blog & Community** (Weeks 11-13)
- Blog content migration
- Community site assessment and implementation

**Phase 5: Testing & Launch** (Weeks 14-16)
- Comprehensive testing (functional, performance, accessibility, SEO)
- URL redirect implementation
- Staging review and production launch
- Post-launch monitoring

## Risks & Mitigation

### High-Priority Risks

**1. Large Volume of Pages**
- **Risk**: 500+ pages is significant undertaking
- **Impact**: High (timeline, quality)
- **Mitigation**: Batch processing, prioritization, parallel work

**2. Complex Form Integrations**
- **Risk**: Forms may break during migration
- **Impact**: High (critical functionality)
- **Mitigation**: Early testing, phased rollout, rollback plan

**3. SEO Impact**
- **Risk**: Traffic loss if redirects fail
- **Impact**: High (business impact)
- **Mitigation**: Comprehensive redirect strategy, monitoring, quick response

**4. Community Forum Complexity**
- **Risk**: Community migration may be too complex
- **Impact**: Medium (separate site possible)
- **Mitigation**: Early assessment, keep separate if needed

### Risk Mitigation Strategy

- Pilot phase validates workflow before bulk migration
- Daily progress tracking with early issue detection
- Quality gates prevent defects from reaching production
- Stakeholder reviews at phase gates
- Rollback plan if critical issues arise

### Go/No-Go Criteria (Week 4 - Pilot Phase)

**Go Decision**: Proceed to bulk migration if:
- ✅ 15-20 pilot pages successfully migrated
- ✅ Quality gates validated and effective
- ✅ Team trained and confident
- ✅ Custom blocks (if needed) functional
- ✅ No major blockers identified

**No-Go Decision**: Pause and reassess if:
- ❌ Quality issues in >20% of pilot pages
- ❌ Major technical blockers unresolved
- ❌ Timeline concerns (velocity too low)
- ❌ Stakeholder concerns not addressed

## Recommendations

### Recommended Approach

1. **Approve 16-Week Migration Plan**: Comprehensive yet achievable timeline
2. **Commit Resources**: 4-person core team essential for success
3. **Pilot First**: De-risk with 15-20 page pilot before full commitment
4. **Prioritize Quality**: Six quality gates prevent issues from reaching production
5. **Phased Launch Option**: Consider launching main site first, then blog/community

### Critical Success Factors

1. **Stakeholder Alignment**: Clear communication and expectations
2. **Resource Commitment**: Full-time team availability
3. **Quality Focus**: Don't sacrifice quality for speed
4. **Early Issue Detection**: Daily tracking and weekly reviews
5. **Flexibility**: Be ready to adjust approach based on learnings

### Alternative Approaches Considered

**Fast Track (10 weeks)**:
- ❌ Not recommended
- Risk: Quality issues, technical debt
- Only viable if scope significantly reduced

**Extended Timeline (20+ weeks)**:
- ✅ Viable if resources limited
- Pro: More thorough testing, less pressure
- Con: Longer time to realize benefits

**Phased Launch**:
- ✅ Recommended option
- Phase 1: Main site + critical pages (12 weeks)
- Phase 2: Blog + community (4 weeks)
- Pro: Faster time to value, risk mitigation
